Who Am I?

Welcome to my website! I am Anton Rotach, a highly motivated and results-driven financial professional with a passion for quantitative finance and data science. Armed with a Master of Science in Financial Analytics and a Bachelor of Science in Quantitative Finance from the esteemed Stevens Institute of Technology, I entered into the world of quantitative analysis.

My expertise lies in the intersection of finance and technology, and I am well-versed in programming languages such as Python, R, and SQL. Additionally, I am proficient in various software and tools, including Tableau, and Bloomberg Terminal, where I am BMC certified. My commitment to professional development is demonstrated by my completion of CFA Level 1, enhancing my knowledge and skills to thrive in the finance industry.

With a solid educational background and a passion for innovation, I have gained valuable hands-on experience as a Junior Modeling Quant at Option Metrics. Here, I honed my skills in Python, developing and enhancing intricate financial models that output option volatilities, Greeks, and volatility surfaces. I am particularly proud of my contribution to the groundbreaking work on the Implied Betting Against Beta factor, which you can read about on the right. Prior to that, at Bloomberg, I leveraged my expertise in Python to clean, process, and analyze earnings data for a diverse portfolio of tech companies. My machine learning models played a vital role in ensuring data accuracy and quality.

Outside of my professional endeavors, I am an avid enthusiast of competitive sports and have actively engaged in swimming and baseball throughout my educational journey. For five years, I was a proud member of a competitive swim team, FOBY, where I honed my skills and nurtured a strong sense of discipline and teamwork. Simultaneously, I channeled my passion for baseball by joining the Marlboro All Star team, where our collective dedication and hard work led us to become State Champions in 2015. In my personal time, I continue to explore and experiment with trading algorithms, always seeking new ways to harness data-driven insights for informed investment decisions. The joy of uncovering valuable patterns and trends within financial and alternative data fuels my dedication to lifelong learning in the dynamic and rewarding field of algorithmic trading.

Teaching an Old Ratio New Tricks


Creating a trading strategy using the Put/Call ratio is old news. This paper reveals why the Put/Call ratio is losing its edge and unveils an exciting enhancement to the strategy with OptionMetrics' Signed Volume data.

Examining the Response of Bond Spreads to Treasury Volatility


The uncertain outlook for inflation and Fed policy coupled with the recent banking crisis has caused Treasury yields to fluctuate significantly in 2023. This article examines the relationship between Treasury volatility and credit spreads, and how it compares to the historical relationship of these measures during economic uncertainty.

0DTE Options: Institutions Get Short

High Year-End Expectations: Should You Count on a Santa Claus Rally?


This article focuses on the Santa Rally and tax loss harvesting phenomena by examining equity returns by month. This analysis is extended into the options market by studying the Volatility Risk Premium ("VRP"), leading to some surprising discoveries.

What is the Best Day to Buy Stocks and Options?

Demand for Consumer Discretionary Stocks: Could They Signify a Market Bottom?


This article looks at investor demand for call and put options on the consumer discretionary fund (XLY). The decrease in the demand for put options may be a sign that investors no longer want to bet against the underperforming sector, indicating that this sector and the market as a whole are looking to bounce back.
