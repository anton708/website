Who Am I?

I was born in St. Petersburg, Russia and moved to the United States at a young age. I went to High School at the Marlboro Business Learning Center where I acquired my passion for business and finance. I am now pursuing these interests as I am currently a Quantitative Finance major at Stevens Institute of Technology. I am looking for a full time position as I am nearing the end of my studies. Quantitative Finance allows me to choose from many career paths that I am interested in such as financial analysis, quantitative research (Equity and fixed income), and risk management.

My athletics throughout my academics include competitive swimming and baseball. I also enjoy the investing competitions created by the organizations (Phi Beta Lambda) I am a part of. They are market simulations where teams have to implement an investing strategy and come out with the highest return. I have made multiple algorithmic trading strategies in python that involve extracting data from an API and calculating different factors. This factor model would then output the daily top investment opportunities. I use websites like Quantopian to backtest my algorithms.

I look to further develop my knowledge in predictive modeling, machine learning, and/or data analysis with companies I am a part of in the future.

Extracurriculars

The most enjoyable organization I am a part of is Future Business Leaders of America (ΦΒΛ). I was in this organization in High School and continue to participate as a college student. There are regional, state, and national competitions which teams had to progress through by taking an assessment, as well as presenting to judges about their topic. I became very interested in the competition and the opportunity to attend a national round where I could meet people from across the country with the same passions as me, so I assembled a team that could compete. We all agreed on a topic that we had knowledge in and had a passion for, Sports and Entertainment Management, which had to do with venue management and sales analysis. Having made it to the state competition, our team placed third in the State of New Jersey and we were asked to attend the national round in Chicago. We took the opportunity and got to learn a lot from the event as well as being able to meet new people and make new connections.
